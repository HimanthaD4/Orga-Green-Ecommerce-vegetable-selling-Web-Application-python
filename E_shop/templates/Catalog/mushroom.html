How to Grow Mushrooms

Grow Guide #2254

Family: Fungi

Genus: Agaricus

Species: Agaricus bisporus

When to Grow Mushrooms

Mushrooms can be grown indoors year-round in controlled environments, or outdoors in suitable climates.

Preparation

Choose a suitable location for growing mushrooms. Indoors, this could be a dark, cool room like a basement or garage. Outdoors, choose a shady spot with indirect sunlight.

Prepare mushroom substrate, such as compost, straw, or sawdust, depending on the type of mushrooms you're growing.

Obtain mushroom spawn or culture from a reputable supplier.

How to Grow Mushrooms

Prepare the mushroom substrate by pasteurizing or sterilizing it to kill off any competing organisms.

Mix the mushroom spawn or culture into the substrate material.

Transfer the substrate mixture into growing containers, such as trays or bags, and seal them to maintain humidity.

Incubate the containers in a dark, warm area until mycelium growth is visible.

Introduce fresh air and reduce humidity levels to induce fruiting.

Harvest mushrooms when they reach the desired size by gently twisting or cutting them from the substrate.

Additional Tips:

  • Maintain proper humidity levels throughout the growing process to prevent drying out or mold growth.
  • Monitor temperature and ventilation to ensure optimal conditions for mushroom growth.
  • Rotate growing containers regularly to promote even growth.

Common Mushroom Varieties

Some common varieties of mushrooms that can be grown include:

  • Button Mushrooms (Agaricus bisporus)
  • Oyster Mushrooms (Pleurotus ostreatus)
  • Shiitake Mushrooms (Lentinula edodes)
  • Portobello Mushrooms (Agaricus bisporus)

How to Store Mushrooms

Store harvested mushrooms in a cool, dark place such as the refrigerator.

Use mushrooms within a few days for best flavor and quality.
