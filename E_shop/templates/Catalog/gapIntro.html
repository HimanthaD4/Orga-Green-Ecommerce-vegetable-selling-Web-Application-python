Here are the products that have received the SL-GAP certificate from the Department of Agriculture that they are produced under the SL-GAP standard. This gives assurance to the consumer about the quality and health safety of these products.

    • Minimizing the health risks that may arise from agricultural products by using agricultural products such as vegetables and fruits that are reliably produced in a quality and safe manner.
    • The birth of a healthy future generation in the use of food without agrochemical residues.
    • By reducing the use of unnecessary inputs in good management, production costs can be reduced and profits can be increased.

Basic activities you need to do in running a farm under GAP system

Mainly follow the following points and related information

You can easily become a GAP grower by keeping records


  1. 1.Site for Farm: Selection of a site with appropriate legal clearance recommended for crop cultivation in a well-rounded area with dimensional boundaries.
  2. 2.Cultivation Environment: Taking appropriate measures to prevent physical, chemical or biological contamination of produce. Under this, confirmation that the soil and water in the farm field are not in polluted condition. Proper disposal of farm crop residues and waste water. Waste should be properly managed
  3. 3. Maintenance of farm structures: All farm structures including soil conservation, efficient water management, structure management including farm storage and harvest handling stations should be properly maintained according to accepted methods.
  4. 4.Farm maintenance activities: Maintain and maintain the farm premises and farm machinery as per the accepted methods of the Department of Agriculture.
  5. 5.Crop management activities: Integrated pest and nutrient management should be followed using recommended seed planting materials, cultivation methods and inputs, pesticides and fertilizers should be used as recommended only in case of need and measures should be taken to get an optimum yield to minimize post-harvest damage.
  6. 6.Maintaining farm information records: Records should be kept for all related activities to verify the above activities, trace the information and perform certification activities based on the data
  7. 7.Work to maintain employee safety and welfare: Adequate facilities should be provided for the welfare and hygiene of all the people involved in the production process.
